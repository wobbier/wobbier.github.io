Not every report ages like the Brawl Stars one. This post exists to show what the disclosure banner looks like when somebody actually ships a fix — consider it the control group.

The Report

Filed July 6th: this blog could count how long a UX complaint sat unfixed, but had no way to celebrate when one actually got fixed. The red doom counter just ticked up forever. Scandalous, frankly.

The Patch

Seven days later, the banner learned the difference. Every disclosure post tracks two dates in its front matter: 
reportDate: "2026-07-06"
fixedDate: "2026-07-13"
While only reportDate is set, the post wears the red UNPATCHED banner and the day count climbs on its own, every day, no rebuild required. The moment a fix ships, adding fixedDate flips it to the green badge you see at the top of this post — days-to-fix stat, receipts and all.
Reported, acknowledged, fixed, seven days. That's the whole lifecycle. If only every report closed this fast — looking at you, Supercell.
